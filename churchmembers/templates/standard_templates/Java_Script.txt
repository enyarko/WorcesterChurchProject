My recommendation for you

Because you're building real business applications, learn JavaScript by applying each topic to your own projects instead of isolated exercises. For example:

Practice DOM manipulation by updating your E-Digit homepage.
Practice events by making your navigation bar and carousel interactive.
Practice form validation with your church member registration form.
Practice Fetch API by loading student or member records from Django.
Practice objects and arrays by managing lists of courses, clients, or church members.

This project-based approach will reinforce the concepts while producing software you can actually use and showcase.

Datatables 

Since you're building church management systems, student management systems, client management systems, and business dashboards, DataTables is one of the most useful frontend libraries you can learn.

Think of DataTables as Bootstrap for data tables.

Why DataTables?

Suppose you have 5 church members.

A normal HTML table works fine.

+------------------------------------------------------+
| Name      | Phone      | Email       | Status        |
+------------------------------------------------------+
| John      | 508...     | john...     | Active        |
| Mary      | 774...     | mary...     | Active        |
| David     | 617...     | david...    | Visitor       |
+------------------------------------------------------+

But imagine your church grows to 5,000 members.

Now users need to:

Search for members
Sort by last name
Find visitors
Filter by ministry
View 25 members per page
Export the table to Excel

A normal HTML table cannot do that by itself.

That's where DataTables comes in.

HTML Table

You first create a normal HTML table.

<table id="members">

What You Should Learn

After understanding a basic DataTable, explore these features in order:

Basic initialization (new DataTable(...) or the jQuery equivalent, depending on the version you use).
Searching and sorting.
Pagination.
Responsive tables.
Export buttons (Excel, PDF, CSV, Print).
Custom column formatting.
Server-side processing for very large datasets.
Integration with Django so your tables display data from your database.
Where You'll Use It in E-Digit Systems

You'll likely use DataTables in almost every business application you build:

Application	How DataTables Helps
Church Management System	Search, filter, and export member records
Visitor Management	Find visitors and attendance history
E-Digit Institute	Manage students, courses, and enrollments
CRM	Track clients, projects, and contacts
Inventory System	View products, stock levels, and suppliers
Accounting System	Display invoices, payments, and transactions
HR System	Manage employees, departments, and leave records

For administrative systems with lots of tabular data, DataTables is one of the quickest ways to give users a professional experience without having to build search, sorting, and pagination from scratch.
